
mv Command

The mv command in Unix/Linux is used to move or rename files and directories. Here are some common examples:

Move a file:
mv source_file target_file

Example: Move file.txt to /new_directory/:
mv file.txt /new_directory/

Rename a file:
mv old_filename new_filename

Example: Rename oldname.txt to newname.txt:
mv oldname.txt newname.txt

Move multiple files to a directory:
mv file1 file2 file3 /target_directory/

Example: Move file1.txt, file2.txt, and file3.txt to /new_directory/:
mv file1.txt file2.txt file3.txt /new_directory/

Move directories:
mv source_directory target_directory

Example: Move /dir1 to /new_directory/dir1:
mv /dir1 /new_directory/

Rename a directory:
mv old_directory new_directory

Example: Rename /old_directory to /new_directory:
mv /old_directory /new_directory
